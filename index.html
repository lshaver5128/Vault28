How it works

Getting a cash offer on your sports card collection is simple, secure, and completely free.

1

Submit details

Use our simple online Sell Your Collection form. Tell us what sports cards you have, approximate counts, condition highlights, and upload photos or videos. Listing highlights helps us value your collection faster.

2

Expert valuation

Our specialists review your submission. We look up recent market sales, PSA price guides, and graded slab comparables. We catalog highlight items into custom graded card preview slabs on your portal.

3

Negotiate & align

You will receive an offer on your dashboard. From there, you can open our built-in real-time live chat to negotiate directly with our buyer, ask questions, explain card highlights, or finalize the offer details.

4

Secure handoff

Once an agreement is reached, choose your handoff method: drop off or ship your cards securely using trackable insured mail, or schedule a safe local meetup if you live near Summerville, South Carolina.

5

Instant payout

Upon receiving and verifying your collection against the submission sheet, we send your payment instantly. Choose PayPal, Venmo, or direct Bank Wire for a quick, hassle-free payout.

🛠️ Connect Google Firebase

Step 1: Go to Firebase Console Open console.firebase.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
Step 2: Create a Project Click Add Project, name it (e.g. Vault28), disable Google Analytics (recommended for fast setup), and click Create.
Step 3: Register a Web App In your project overview, click the Web icon (</>) to register an app. Name it Vault28Web and click register.
Step 4: Copy Config Keys Firebase will show you a configuration script. Copy only the firebaseConfig object. It looks like this: 
const firebaseConfig = {
  apiKey: "AIzaSy...",
  authDomain: "vault28.firebaseapp.com",
  projectId: "vault28",
  storageBucket: "vault28.appspot.com",
  messagingSenderId: "123456...",
  appId: "1:123456..."
};
Step 5: Paste in app.js Open app.js in VS Code and paste your config into the FIREBASE_CONFIG variable at the very top of the file. Save and push the change to your GitHub repo!
